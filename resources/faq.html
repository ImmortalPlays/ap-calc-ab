FAQ

The questions students ask most about the AP Calculus AB exam. Tap a question to expand the answer.

▶What score do I need?

The exam is scored 1–5. A 3 is "qualified" and is generally considered passing; many colleges grant credit for a 4 or 5, and some accept a 3. Always check the specific policy of the college you're aiming for.

▶How is the exam scored?

Two sections — Multiple Choice and Free Response — each worth 50%. Your raw points combine into a composite score, which is then converted to the final 1–5 scale.

▶What's the difference between AB and BC?

AB covers roughly the first two semesters of college calculus — limits, derivatives and integrals. BC includes everything in AB plus extra topics like series, parametric and polar functions. This site covers AB.

▶How long is it, and when do I take it?

About 3 hours 15 minutes: 45 multiple-choice questions (1h 45m) and 6 free-response questions (1h 30m). It's held in early-to-mid May — College Board sets the exact date each year.

▶Do I need to memorize formulas?

Yes — no formula sheet is provided. Know the derivatives and integrals of the common functions, plus the big theorems. The Cheat Sheet on this site collects the ones worth memorizing.

▶What should I bring on exam day?

An approved graphing calculator (plus fresh backup batteries), several No. 2 pencils and a couple of pens, and whatever ID / admission materials your school requires. Get there early.
