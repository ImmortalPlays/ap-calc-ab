Calculator Guide

On the exam you switch between calculator and no-calculator sections. Here's exactly when it's allowed — and how to use it without losing points.

When can you use it?

CALCULATOR ALLOWED
  • Multiple Choice — Part B
  • Free Response — Part A
NO CALCULATOR
  • Multiple Choice — Part A
  • Free Response — Part B

The 4 things it must do

On calculator sections you're expected to do these four things — no intermediate work required.

1

Graph a function

Plot in any viewing window you choose.

2

Solve equations

Find zeros / where two graphs intersect.

3

Derivative at a point

Evaluate \(f'(a)\) numerically.

4

Definite integral

Evaluate \(\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\) numerically.

Smart habits

  • Keep full decimals in the calculator and round only the final answer — to 3 decimal places.
  • Always write the setup first (the integral, equation or derivative). Graders score the setup, not just the number.
  • Stay in radian mode unless a problem clearly uses degrees.
  • Label what you typed so your work reads clearly: e.g. "\(\int_0^4 v(t)\,dt = 12.5\)".

Common mistakes

  • Being in degree mode for a trig problem — answers come out wrong with no warning.
  • Rounding too early and carrying the rounded number forward.
  • Writing only the final number on a free-response question with no setup shown.
