Calculator Guide

A hands-on guide to the TI-84 Plus / TI-84 Plus CE — the exact keys to press for everything AP Calculus AB asks you to do. If you've never felt comfortable with your calculator, start at the top and work down.

When can you use it?

CALCULATOR ALLOWED
  • Multiple Choice — Part B
  • Free Response — Part A
NO CALCULATOR
  • Multiple Choice — Part A
  • Free Response — Part B

You may bring two approved graphing calculators. The TI-84 family is approved. Clear or reset memory only if your teacher tells you to — you don't need to.

Know your keys

Almost everything in calculus uses just a handful of keys. The blue/green words above a key are reached with the 2ND key first.

Y=

Y=

Type the function(s) you want to graph. Up to 10 (Y1, Y2, …).

WIN

WINDOW

Set the viewing box: Xmin, Xmax, Ymin, Ymax.

ZOOM

ZOOM

6 = standard (−10 to 10). 0 = ZoomFit auto-fits Y.

GRAPH

GRAPH

Draws what's in Y= using the current window.

CALC

2ND → TRACE

Opens the CALC menu — zeros, intersect, dy/dx, ∫. This is the big one.

MATH

MATH

Holds nDeriv( and fnInt( for derivatives & integrals without a graph.

First: set RADIAN mode

Calculus runs on radians. A wrong mode silently gives wrong trig answers, so check this before every exam.

  1. Press MODE.
  2. Arrow down to the RADIAN / DEGREE row.
  3. Highlight RADIAN and press ENTER.
  4. Press 2ND → MODE (QUIT) to leave.

Graph a function, step by step

Say you want to graph \(f(x) = x^3 - 4x\).

  1. Press Y=. Next to Y1= type X^3 − 4X. (The X key is X,T,θ,n, just under MODE. Use the white (−) key for negatives, not the blue minus.)
  2. Press ZOOM then 6 for the standard window. You'll see the curve.
  3. If the graph runs off the top or bottom, press ZOOM then 0 (ZoomFit), or open WINDOW and widen Ymin/Ymax by hand.
  4. Press TRACE and use ◄ ► to walk a blinking dot along the curve and read off coordinates.

Tip: to graph two functions at once (for an intersection), just put one in Y1 and the other in Y2.

The 4 exam skills

On calculator sections you're expected to do these four things directly — no algebra shown. Each opens from the CALC menu (2ND → TRACE). Tap a skill for the full keystrokes.

▶1 · Graph a function & read a value — 1: value
  1. Enter the function in Y1 and press GRAPH.
  2. Press 2ND → TRACE to open CALC, choose 1: value.
  3. Type the \(x\) you want (e.g. 2) and press ENTER.
  4. The calculator marks the point and shows \(y = f(2)\) at the bottom.

Great for "find \(f(2)\)" or checking a point lies on the curve.

▶2 · Solve an equation — zeros & intersections (2: zero, 5: intersect)

To solve \(f(x) = 0\) (find an x-intercept / "zero" / "root"):

  1. Put \(f\) in Y1, press GRAPH.
  2. 2ND → TRACE, choose 2: zero.
  3. Left Bound? — move the cursor just left of the crossing, press ENTER.
  4. Right Bound? — move just right of it, press ENTER.
  5. Guess? — press ENTER again. The zero appears as X=….

To solve \(f(x) = g(x)\) (where two graphs meet):

  1. Put \(f\) in Y1 and \(g\) in Y2, press GRAPH.
  2. 2ND → TRACE, choose 5: intersect.
  3. Press ENTER for First curve?, ENTER for Second curve?, then ENTER for Guess? near the crossing.
  4. The intersection point shows as X=… Y=….

Trick for solving any equation like \(e^x = 3x\): move everything to one side (\(e^x - 3x = 0\)) and use zero, or graph each side and use intersect.

▶3 · Derivative at a point — 6: dy/dx (and MATH nDeriv()

This evaluates \(f'(a)\) — the slope at a point — numerically.

From the graph:

  1. Put \(f\) in Y1, press GRAPH.
  2. 2ND → TRACE, choose 6: dy/dx.
  3. Type the \(x\)-value (e.g. 1) and press ENTER. The slope \(f'(1)\) appears.

From the home screen (no graph needed):

  1. Press MATH, choose 8: nDeriv(.
  2. Fill the template \(\frac{d}{dx}\big(\,\square\,\big)\big|_{x=\square}\): the function, then the value. Example for \(f(x)=x^2\) at \(x=3\): nDeriv(X², X, 3) → 6.

Older OS shows nDeriv(expr, X, a); the CE shows a tidy \(d/dx\) template — same result.

▶4 · Definite integral — 7: ∫f(x)dx (and MATH fnInt()

This evaluates \(\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\) — the (signed) area — numerically.

From the graph:

  1. Put \(f\) in Y1, press GRAPH.
  2. 2ND → TRACE, choose 7: ∫f(x)dx.
  3. Lower Limit? — type \(a\), press ENTER. Upper Limit? — type \(b\), press ENTER.
  4. The region shades and the value of the integral appears.

From the home screen:

  1. Press MATH, choose 9: fnInt(.
  2. Fill \(\displaystyle\int_\square^\square \square\,dx\): lower, upper, function, variable. Example \(\int_0^2 3x^2\,dx\): fnInt(3X², X, 0, 2) → 8.

On a free-response question, still write the integral on paper — e.g. "\(\int_0^4 v(t)\,dt = 12.5\)". The setup earns points, not just the number.

Two more worth knowing

MAX / MIN

In CALC use 3: minimum or 4: maximum exactly like zero (left bound, right bound, guess) to find a peak or valley — handy for optimization checks.

TABLE

Press 2ND → GRAPH to see a table of \(x\) and \(y\) values for whatever's in Y=. Set the start/step with 2ND → WINDOW (TBLSET). Great for estimating limits.

Smart habits

  • Keep full decimals in the calculator and round only the final answer — to 3 decimal places, the AP standard.
  • Always write the setup first (the integral, equation, or derivative). Graders score the setup, not just the number.
  • Stay in radian mode unless a problem clearly uses degrees.
  • Store messy numbers instead of retyping: type a value then STO→ and a letter (e.g. A) to reuse it exactly.
  • Use the white (−) key for negative numbers and the blue − key only for subtraction — mixing them throws a SYNTAX error.

Common mistakes

  • Being in degree mode for a trig problem — answers come out wrong with no warning.
  • Rounding too early and carrying the rounded number forward.
  • Forgetting to close parentheses in nDeriv( / fnInt(.
  • A flat line or nothing on screen usually means a bad WINDOW — try ZOOM 6 then ZOOM 0.
  • Writing only the final number on free response with no setup shown.
