Cheat Sheet

Every formula, rule, and theorem on the AP Calculus AB exam — organised unit by unit. Skim it the night before, or jump to a unit with the search box.

Unit 1 · Limits & Continuity

Evaluating limits

Try substitution first. A real number is the answer.
Form \(\frac{0}{0}\), polynomials: factor & cancel.
Form \(\frac{0}{0}\), radicals: multiply by the conjugate.
Form \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\), rational: divide by the highest power.
Squeeze: if \(g \le f \le h\) and \(\lim g = \lim h = L\), then \(\lim f = L\).

Special limits & properties

\(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\)
\(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\frac{1-\cos x}{x} = 0\)
Limits split across \(+,-,\times,\div\) (denominator \(\neq 0\)).
Two-sided limit exists \(\iff\) left limit \(=\) right limit.

Continuity

Continuous at \(a\) needs all three: \(f(a)\) defined, \(\lim_{x\to a} f\) exists, and \(\lim_{x\to a} f = f(a)\).
Removable (hole): limit exists but \(\neq f(a)\).
Jump: left and right limits differ.
Infinite: vertical asymptote.

Asymptotes & IVT

Horizontal: \(\lim_{x\to\pm\infty} f = L \Rightarrow y = L\). For rationals, compare degrees.
Vertical: where denominator \(= 0\) but numerator \(\neq 0\).
IVT: if \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\), it takes every value between \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\) — used to prove a solution exists.

Unit 2 · Differentiation Basics

The derivative

\(f'(a) = \displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h}\)
Equivalent: \(f'(a) = \displaystyle\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}\)
Meaning: instantaneous rate of change = slope of the tangent line.
Needs left derivative \(=\) right derivative to exist.

Basic rules

Power: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}x^n = n x^{n-1}\)
Constant multiple: \((cf)' = c f'\);   Sum: \((f\pm g)' = f' \pm g'\)
Product: \((uv)' = u'v + uv'\)
Quotient: \(\left(\dfrac{u}{v}\right)' = \dfrac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}\)

Common derivatives

\(\dfrac{d}{dx}e^x = e^x\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln x = \dfrac{1}{x}\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\sin x = \cos x\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\cos x = -\sin x\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\tan x = \sec^2 x\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\sec x = \sec x\tan x\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\cot x = -\csc^2 x\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\csc x = -\csc x\cot x\)

Tangent lines & differentiability

Tangent at \((a, f(a))\): \(y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)\).
Differentiable \(\Rightarrow\) continuous (converse is false).
Not differentiable at corners, cusps, vertical tangents.
\(f''\): concavity & acceleration; \(f' = 0\) means a horizontal tangent.

Unit 3 · Chain, Implicit & Inverse

Chain rule

\(\dfrac{d}{dx}f(g(x)) = f'(g(x))\,g'(x)\) — outside\('\) times inside\('\).
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}u^n = n u^{n-1}u'\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}e^{u} = e^{u}u'\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln u = \dfrac{u'}{u}\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\sin u = \cos u \cdot u'\)

Implicit & log differentiation

Differentiate both sides in \(x\); every \(y\)-term gets a \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\).
Group the \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\) terms, factor, and solve.
Log diff (for \(y = x^x\) etc.): take \(\ln\) of both sides, then differentiate implicitly.

Inverse functions

\(\big(f^{-1}\big)'(a) = \dfrac{1}{f'(b)}\) where \(f(b) = a\).
Slopes of inverses are reciprocals at matching points.
Graphs of \(f\) and \(f^{-1}\) mirror across \(y = x\).

Inverse trig & other bases

\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\arcsin x = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\arccos x = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}\arctan x = \dfrac{1}{1+x^2}\)
\(\dfrac{d}{dx}a^x = a^x\ln a\),   \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\log_a x = \dfrac{1}{x\ln a}\)

Unit 4 · Contextual Applications

Motion

\(v(t) = x'(t)\),   \(a(t) = v'(t)\),   speed \(= |v(t)|\).
At rest when \(v = 0\); direction changes when \(v\) changes sign.
Speeding up when \(v,a\) have the same sign; slowing down when opposite.

Related rates

Draw → label → write a relating equation → differentiate w.r.t. \(t\) → substitute knowns → solve.
Substitute constants after differentiating, not before.
Volumes: sphere \(V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\), cone \(V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\), cylinder \(V = \pi r^2 h\).

L'Hôpital's Rule

If \(\dfrac{f}{g}\to\dfrac{0}{0}\) or \(\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}\): \(\displaystyle\lim\frac{f}{g} = \lim\frac{f'}{g'}\).
Check the form first; it's not the quotient rule.
Other forms (\(0\cdot\infty\), \(\infty-\infty\)) must be rewritten into a ratio first.

Linear approximation

\(L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a)\), the tangent line at \(a\).
Estimate: \(f(a + h) \approx f(a) + f'(a)\cdot h\).
Overestimate if \(f'' < 0\) (concave down); underestimate if \(f'' > 0\).

Unit 5 · Analytical Applications

Critical points & extrema

Critical point: \(f' = 0\) or undefined (and in the domain).
1st-derivative test: \(f'\) goes \(+\to-\) (local max), \(-\to+\) (local min).
2nd-derivative test: at \(f'(c)=0\), \(f''(c)>0\) min, \(f''(c)<0\) max, \(f''(c)=0\) inconclusive.

Concavity & reading graphs

\(f'' > 0\): concave up;   \(f'' < 0\): concave down.
Inflection point where \(f''\) changes sign.
\(f\) increasing where \(f' > 0\); inflection points of \(f\) = local extrema of \(f'\).

Absolute extrema (Candidates Test)

On a closed \([a,b]\), evaluate \(f\) at all critical points and both endpoints.
Largest value = absolute max, smallest = absolute min.
Open intervals have no guarantee — check end behaviour.

MVT & optimization

MVT: continuous on \([a,b]\), differentiable on \((a,b)\) \(\Rightarrow\) some \(c\) with \(f'(c) = \dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\).
Rolle's: MVT when \(f(a) = f(b)\), giving \(f'(c) = 0\).
Optimization: objective + constraint → one variable → set derivative \(= 0\) → justify with a test.

Unit 6 · Integration & Accumulation

Antiderivatives

\(\displaystyle\int x^n\,dx = \dfrac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \;(n\neq -1)\)
\(\displaystyle\int \dfrac{1}{x}\,dx = \ln|x| + C\),   \(\displaystyle\int e^x\,dx = e^x + C\)
\(\displaystyle\int \cos x\,dx = \sin x + C\),   \(\displaystyle\int \sin x\,dx = -\cos x + C\)
Always \(+C\) on indefinite integrals; use an initial condition to find it.

Fundamental Theorem

Part 1: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\displaystyle\int_a^x f(t)\,dt = f(x)\).
With a function on top: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\displaystyle\int_a^{g(x)} f\,dt = f(g(x))\,g'(x)\).
Part 2: \(\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)\).

u-substitution & Riemann sums

Let \(u = g(x)\), \(du = g'(x)\,dx\). Definite: change the bounds via \(u = g(x)\).
Riemann sums: left, right, midpoint, trapezoid, with \(\Delta x = \dfrac{b-a}{n}\).
Trapezoid overestimates when concave up, underestimates when concave down.

Properties & average value

\(\displaystyle\int_a^b f + \int_b^c f = \int_a^c f\);   \(\displaystyle\int_b^a f = -\int_a^b f\);   \(\displaystyle\int_a^a f = 0\).
Average value: \(f_{\text{avg}} = \dfrac{1}{b-a}\displaystyle\int_a^b f\,dx\).
Accumulation: \(A(x) = \displaystyle\int_a^x f(t)\,dt\) — read its graph like a derivative graph.

Unit 7 · Differential Equations

Separable equations

Separate variables → integrate both sides → \(+C\).
Apply the initial condition to find \(C\) before solving for \(y\).
If you can't write it as \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = f(x)g(y)\), it's beyond AB scope.

Growth, decay & half-life

\(\dfrac{dy}{dt} = ky \Rightarrow y = Ce^{kt}\) (\(k>0\) growth, \(k<0\) decay).
Half-life: \(t_{1/2} = \dfrac{\ln 2}{|k|}\);   doubling time: \(T_d = \dfrac{\ln 2}{k}\).
Each half-life multiplies the amount by \(\tfrac12\).

Slope fields

At each \((x,y)\) draw a short segment with slope \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\).
Horizontal segments where \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 0\); steeper = larger slope.
A solution curve must stay tangent to the segments it passes through.

Verifying & Newton's cooling

Verify a solution by substituting \(y\) and \(y'\) into the equation — both sides must match.
Newton's cooling: \(\dfrac{dy}{dt} = k(y - A) \Rightarrow y = A + Ce^{kt}\), equilibrium at \(y = A\).

Unit 8 · Applications of Integration

Motion & net change

Displacement \(= \displaystyle\int_a^b v(t)\,dt\);   total distance \(= \displaystyle\int_a^b |v(t)|\,dt\).
Position: \(x(t) = x(0) + \displaystyle\int_0^t v(s)\,ds\).
Net Change: \(\text{end} = \text{start} + \displaystyle\int (\text{rate in} - \text{rate out})\,dt\).

Area between curves

Vertical strips (\(dx\)): \(\displaystyle\int_a^b [\text{top} - \text{bottom}]\,dx\).
Horizontal strips (\(dy\)): \(\displaystyle\int_c^d [\text{right} - \text{left}]\,dy\).
Find all intersections; split the region where the order swaps.

Volumes of revolution

Disk: \(V = \pi\displaystyle\int_a^b [R(x)]^2\,dx\).
Washer: \(V = \pi\displaystyle\int_a^b \big([R]^2 - [r]^2\big)\,dx\) (\(R\) outer, \(r\) inner).
Trap: \([R]^2 - [r]^2\), never \((R - r)^2\) — square each radius separately.

Volumes by cross-section

\(V = \displaystyle\int_a^b A(x)\,dx\), with side \(s = \text{top} - \text{bottom}\).
Square: \(A = s^2\);   semicircle (diameter \(s\)): \(A = \dfrac{\pi}{8}s^2\).
Equilateral triangle: \(A = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2\);   isosceles right (leg \(s\)): \(A = \dfrac{1}{2}s^2\).

Exam structure

The 3 hr 15 min exam

Section I — Multiple Choice (1 hr 45 min, 50%): Part A 30 questions no calculator, Part B 15 questions calculator.
Section II — Free Response (1 hr 30 min, 50%): Part A 2 questions calculator, Part B 4 questions no calculator.
A formula sheet is not provided — memorize what's here.

Free-response habits

Justify with calculus (derivative tests, EVT, IVT) — "it looks like a max" earns nothing.
State theorem hypotheses (continuous / differentiable) before using MVT or IVT.
For \(\frac{0}{0}\) limits, never write \(\frac{f}{g} = \frac{f'}{g'}\) flatly — show the form or the separate limits.
Keep full decimals; round only the final answer to 3 places. Include units.
